## Primary Reference

The following paper is the **primary citation** for MHASpread. Please cite it whenever you use the model in research or training.

<div class="pub-card">
  <div class="pub-authors">Cardenas, N. C., Lopes, F. P. N., Machado, A., Maran, V., Trois, C., Machado, F. A., & Machado, G.</div>
  <div class="pub-title">Modeling foot-and-mouth disease dissemination in Rio Grande do Sul, Brazil and evaluating the effectiveness of control measures.</div>
  <div class="pub-journal">Frontiers in Veterinary Science, 11 (2024), 1468864.</div>
  <div class="pub-doi"><a href="https://doi.org/10.3389/fvets.2024.1468864">doi:10.3389/fvets.2024.1468864</a></div>
</div>

**Contribution to MHASpread**: This paper introduces the MHASpread framework and provides its primary scientific validation. It demonstrates how the stochastic multiscale model reproduces observed outbreak dynamics and evaluates the comparative effectiveness of depopulation, ring vaccination, and movement standstill strategies.
