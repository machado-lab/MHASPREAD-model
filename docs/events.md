# Events and Training

MHASpread has been instrumental in international capacity-building and training initiatives aimed at strengthening disease surveillance and epidemiological modeling expertise among veterinary and public health authorities in Latin America and beyond.

---

## Overview

The MHASpread framework provides a powerful platform for:

- **Hands-on training** in stochastic multiscale disease modeling
- **Policy evaluation** through scenario-based simulation
- **International collaboration** on transboundary animal disease preparedness
- **Knowledge transfer** to next-generation disease control experts

---

## Chile FMD Workshop 2024

**Event**: Chile FMD Simulation Modeling Workshop  
**URL**: [workshop_aftosa_chile_2024](https://machado-lab.github.io/workshop_aftosa_chile_2024/)  
**Date**: 2024  
**Host**: Chilean National Agricultural Service (SAG)

### Overview

A comprehensive training program for Chilean national animal health authorities focused on FMD simulation modeling, risk-based surveillance design, and emergency response preparedness.

### Target Audience

- National veterinary authorities (SAG epidemiologists)
- Regional livestock officials
- Emergency response coordinators
- Researchers in veterinary epidemiology

### Training Focus

1. **FMD Epidemiology in Practice**: South American context, endemic/sporadic regions
2. **Modeling Foundations**: SEIR compartmental models, stochastic simulation
3. **MHASpread Practical Session**: Running simulations with Chilean farm networks
4. **Control Strategy Evaluation**: Depopulation vs. vaccination trade-offs
5. **Cost-Effectiveness Analysis**: Economic integration of epidemiological models
6. **Scenario Planning**: Designing contingency strategies for FMD entry

### Participant Outcomes

Participants gained:

- **Conceptual mastery**: Understanding multi-host disease dynamics
- **Technical skills**: R-based simulation running and result visualization
- **Policy insight**: Evidence-based evaluation of control options
- **Networking**: Regional connections with FMD research community

### Resources

- Live modeling demonstrations
- Worked examples using Chilean livestock data
- Download tutorials at workshop repository

## PANAFTOSA Workshop Rio 2023

**Event**: Pan American Animal Health Organization (PAHO) Regional Workshop  
**URL**: [PANAFTOSA-Workshop-Rio2023](https://machado-lab.github.io/PANAFTOSA-Workshop-Rio2023/)  
**Date**: 2023  
**Host**: PAHO Regional Office, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

### Overview

A landmark regional capacity-building initiative bringing together epidemiologists and veterinary officials from across the Americas to advance collaborative approaches to transboundary animal disease modeling and control.

### Participating Countries

- Brazil (host)
- Argentina
- Bolivia
- Chile
- Colombia
- Ecuador
- Paraguay
- Uruguay
- Other regional partners

### Training Focus

1. **Transboundary FMD**:  Epidemiology of multi-country outbreaks, trade routes
2. **Metapopulation Modeling**: Network-level disease dynamics across regions
3. **Stochastic Uncertainty**: Quantifying model and parameter uncertainty
4. **Joint Preparedness**: Coordinated response, data sharing, information exchange
5. **MHASpread Application**: Case studies from region and risk scenarios
6. **Policy Integration**: Balancing agricultural trade with disease prevention

### Collaborative Themes

- **Data harmonization**: Standardizing livestock databases across nations
- **Early warning systems**: Detecting spatial spread via shared surveillance
- **Coordinated response**: Timely information exchange and control alignment
- **Capacity sustainability**: Building local expertise for ongoing improvements

### Workshop Materials

- Case studies from 2000–2001 FMD outbreak in South America
- Worked examples from diverse agroecological zones
- Regional trade network analysis
- Collaborative simulation exercises

## PAHO MHASpread Workshop Repository

**Repository**: [MHASpread_workshop_PAHO](https://github.com/machado-lab/MHASpread_workshop_PAHO/)  
**Type**: Open-access educational materials  
**Access**: Freely available on GitHub

### Contents

#### Tutorials

Step-by-step guides covering:

1. **Setting up simulation environment**: R packages, data import
2. **Preparing livestock data**: Population and movement databases
3. **Running baseline scenario**: Uncontrolled epidemic simulation
4. **Control strategy scenarios**: Depopulation, vaccination, standstill
5. **Result visualization**: Epidemic curves, spatial patterns, outcomes
6. **Cost-effectiveness analysis**: Economic integration and comparison

#### Datasets

Example livestock databases from:

- Brazilian agricultural regions
- Bolivian mixed farming systems
- Chilean livestock networks

Datasets demonstrate:

- **Realistic complexity**: Real farm distributions, movement patterns
- **Diverse contexts**: Large commercial vs. smallholder systems
- **Reproducibility**: Exactly duplicate published results

#### Worked Examples

Complete R scripts demonstrating:

- **Basic simulation**: Running MHASpread with standard parameters
- **Scenario comparison**: Multiple control strategies side-by-side
- **Sensitivity analysis**: Varying key assumptions
- **Ensemble analysis**: Aggregating results across 100+ replicates

#### Case Studies

Real-world applications described in peer-reviewed literature:

1. **Brazil (Rio Grande do Sul)**: Historical outbreak reconstruction, control efficacy
2. **Bolivia**: Control strategy evaluation in resource-constrained context
3. **Regional comparison**: Adaptation of model to different agroecological zones

#### Video Demonstrations

Screen-capture tutorials demonstrating:

- Loading data and running simulations
- Interpreting outputs and generating figures
- Troubleshooting common issues

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I access workshop materials?

All materials are freely available on GitHub (links at top of page). Download, fork, or clone repositories.

### Can I run MHASpread without attending a workshop?

**Yes!** Materials are self-contained. Start with README files and example scripts. Seek help via GitHub issues or contact developers.

### Do I need to know R to participate?

**No prior R experience needed** for basic workshops. Intermediate/advanced sessions assume some familiarity. Learning resources provided.

### How often are workshops held?

Depends on partner institutions. Ad-hoc workshops arranged as requested. Check repositories for scheduled events.

### Can materials be translated to my language?

Community contributions welcome! Contact Machado Lab about translation partnerships.
